Tommy Tang, a master's student
studying computer science
at Stanford University.

I am a software engineer and project manager interested in game development.

Currently, I am an officer for the Stanford Video Game Developers Association, in charge of hosting events with company partners and organizing game jams for students. (Please reach out if you would like to with us!) At the same time, I am working on a new game for the game design course I am part of this semester. Prevously, I developed Red or Blue, a 2D puzzle platformer with a unique color-switching mechanic, which was a finalist for showcase at Boston Festival of Indie Games 2018.
Outside of game development, my proudest accomplishment is leading a team in building a sophisticated mobile app for the Harvard Square Homeless Shelter. In addition, I am passionate about teaching CS, having been the assistant director for Harvard iD Tech Camp this summer and a long-time teaching assistant for courses such as Algorithms and Programming Languages.
Beyond CS, I was one of the organizers of TEDxTufts. I spend a lot of my free time playing video games (my favorite game is Hollow Knight), reading (my favorite series is The Kingkiller Chronicle), and playing DnD.

Recent Work (additional school projects available upon request)

Harvard Square Homeless Shelter App

I led a team of 12 students in developing a mobile app using React Native, Redux and Firebase. It enables users to view and log interactions with guests, maintain a shared to-do list, and much more.

Bullet Heaven (Coming Soon)

I am working on a melee-based bullet-hell game in Unity where the player has to dash and dodge his way to victory. Contributing enemy and boss design + implementation.

Red or Blue

I designed and built a 2D puzzle platformer in Unity where platforms, enemies and traps are either colored red or blue, and only one color is visible and can be interacted with at once. (codebase)

Microsoft Avatar (Finished top 10 in the Millennials category at 2017 Microsoft Global Hackathon)

At Microsoft Research, I led a team of five in creating a style-transfer web app based on Deep Image Analogy technique. It enables users to create stylized digital avatars using any selfie. I planned the project from scratch and designed and implemented the front-end using React. (No public demo available)

In depth analysis of the Great Firewall of China

As a research project, I studied the censorship techniques of the GFW and potential countermeasures, using captured packets for experiment.

MyDea

I created an Android app that lets users share ideas for projects they are interested in, which other users can view, comment, vote on, and join. Used Node.js and PostgreSQL for backend. (codebase)

Drizzle (Winner of Bose Prize at PolyHack 2016)

I made a web app using Python, Django, D3 JS Library, and the Bose SoundTouch API that enables users to mix and create customized ambient noise tracks with an innovative clock-like interface.
